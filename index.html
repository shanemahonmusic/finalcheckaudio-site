Technical

Built on real mastering standards

Final Check combines established loudness and true peak standards with additional signal integrity checks used in professional delivery workflows.

Integrated loudness (LUFS) and loudness range (LRA) follow the EBU R128 / ITU-R BS.1770-4 loudness model.

True peak levels are measured using oversampled inter-sample peak detection, allowing the system to identify peaks that may occur between digital samples during playback.

All analysis runs locally on your computer. Audio files are never uploaded, stored, or transmitted.

FAQ

What operating systems are supported?

Available for macOS and Windows.

macOS:
macOS 13.7.8 and later. Tested on Intel and Apple Silicon (M1/M2/M3).

Windows:
Available in both 32-bit and 64-bit versions.

Most users should download the 64-bit version (standard on modern systems). The 32-bit version is only for older Windows computers.

If you're unsure, choose 64-bit as it's correct for nearly all users. If you download the wrong version, you can re-download the correct one using the link in your purchase email.

On some systems, Windows may display a security prompt the first time you open the app. This is normal for new software and you can proceed to run it.

Does MVP Final Check judge creative quality or whether a track sounds “good”?

No. MVP Final Check assesses technical readiness only. It does not judge creative quality, taste, songwriting, arrangement, or artistic value. It helps you decide whether a premaster or master is technically ready to send out and highlights areas to review if it isn’t.

Is this a plugin?

No. It’s a standalone desktop app, not a VST, AU, or AAX plugin. Export your audio from your DAW and run the check inside the app.

What does this actually tell me?

It tells you whether your premaster or master is ready to send out, based on loudness, dynamics, peak behaviour, and additional signal checks, and shows what to fix next if it isn’t.

What do PASS / CAUTION / FAIL actually mean?

PASS means your file is in a healthy range for the selected check. CAUTION means there are issues worth reviewing before delivery. FAIL means it likely needs changes before you send it to DJs, labels, or a mastering engineer.

Is this a replacement for a mastering engineer?

No. Final Check is a decision tool, not a mastering service. It helps you verify whether a premaster or master is ready before sending it out.

Does it require an internet connection?

No. The app runs locally on your computer. Internet is only required once during licence activation.

How do I receive access after purchase?

After purchase, you’ll receive your licence key and download instructions by email. Enter the key in the app to activate your copy.

What file types can I analyse?

The app supports common production formats such as WAV and AIFF exported from your DAW.

Can I install it on multiple machines?

Each licence allows activation on a limited number of devices. If you change computers, contact support and we’ll help with activation management.

Who is this for?

Final Check is built for Producers, Mastering Engineers, Record Labels, Artist Managers, Audio Professionals, Music Students and Educators who want a fast confidence check before release or delivery.
